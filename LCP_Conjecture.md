# Leech Lattice-Periodic Table Correspondence Conjecture (LCP Conjecture)

## Conjecture Statement

Let $\Lambda_{24} \subset \mathbb{R}^{24}$ be the 24-dimensional Leech lattice, with kissing number
$$
K_{24} = 196560 = 2^4 \times 3^3 \times 5 \times 7 \times 13
$$

### Conjecture 1 (Electron Shell Capacity Conjecture)

For angular quantum number $l \in \{0,1,2,3,4\}$ (corresponding to s, p, d, f, g orbitals), the electron shell capacity $C_l = 2(2l+1)$ satisfies:
$C_l \mid 196560$
and for $l=5$ (h orbital), $C_5 = 22 = 2 \times 11 \nmid 196560$.

### Conjecture 2 (Periodic Structure Conjecture)

The length $L_n$ of the $n$-th period in the periodic table can be expressed as:
$$
L_n = \sum_{l=0}^{k} 2(2l+1)
$$
where $k = \lfloor (n-1)/2 \rfloor$, and each summation term is a divisor of 196560.

### Conjecture 3 (Periodic Table Completeness Conjecture)

The chemical element periodic table terminates at atomic number $Z = 138$, meaning no stable elements in the 9th period exist. Equivalent statement: no stable h orbital ($l=5$) electron shell exists.
